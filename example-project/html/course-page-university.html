This course considers some of the most pressing local and global issues of the day and explores elements of social change that work towards building an ever-advancing civilization from a Baha’i perspective. This course engages students in examination of interconnected social, economic, political and environmental, issues coupled with interdisciplinary and holistic solutions. Research conducted in the course will be relevant to the needs of students and their vision for individual and/or collective changemaking. It examines the Bahá’í view of history, its understanding of the role of religion in social evolution, its conceptualization of the direction of social progress and how the Bahá’ís are engaging in social action at present using the tools of education and training, reflection, service to humanity, and consultation.

Topics
  • Power, politics, governance and law
  • Economics, work and business, poverty eradication and wealth
  • Human rights, gender equality
  • Justice, eliminating prejudice
  • The environment
  • Unity, peace, development
  • Education, service to humanity, consultation
Who is the course for?
Who is the course for?

Undergraduate and graduate students interested in a holistic perspective on social change. Persons interested in auditing the course should contact the Director.

What will you achieve?
You will learn
Ability to think critically and analyze the interconnected nature of social problems that necessitate holistic approaches for sustained solutions.
Research mulit-faceted problems from both scientific and spiritual perspectives that offer new ways to conceptualize social change.
Utilize knowledge from course materials to apply to the participants’ reality and goals for changemaking.
Increase communication skills through written and oral presentations/projects on relevant topics selected by the student.
Collaborate with others to embark on applying concepts from the course to changemaking at the grassroots.
